50 years of monopoles in gauge theory

This conference is in part being held to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the seminal papers by ‘t Hooft and Polyakov introducing monopoles in gague theory. The original papers can be found here:

G. ‘t Hooft, Magnetic monopoles in unified theories, Nucl. Phys. B, (1974)

A. M. Polyakov, Particle spectrum in quantum field theory, JETP Lett. 20, 194 (1974).

Super-Yang-Mills theory

One major breakthrough was Seiberg-Witten theory, which studies the moduli space of vacua in N=2 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory. They also demsontrate that confinement can be demonstrated via monopole condensation.

N Seiberg, E Witten, Monopoles, duality and chiral symmetry breaking in N=2 supersymmetric QCD, Nucl. Phys. B 431:484-550 (1994)

N Seiberg, E Witten, Monopole Condensation, And Confinement In N=2 Supersymmetric Yang-Mills Theory, Nucl. Phys. B 426:19-52 (1994)

Milestones in the history of monopoles

2024 marks fifty years since the discovery of smooth monopoles in nonabelian gauge theories. Below is a history of milestones in the development of the subject since its inception. This page is not large enough to accommodate all of the many contributions on this topic; the authors have endeavoured to highlight developments that had a lasting influence on the subject.

  • 1974

    't Hooft and Polyakov independently discover smooth monopoles in nonabelian gauge theories. Polyakov's paper placed them in a broader framework of topological solitons, while 't Hooft's paper highlighted their similarity with Dirac's magnetic monopoles.
    G. 't Hooft, Magnetic monopolesin unified gauge theories, Nuclear Physics B79 (1974) 276-284
    A.M. Polyakov, Particle spectrum in quantum field theory, ZhETF Pis. Red. 20, No. 6, 430-433.

  • 1975

    Prasad and Sommerfield discover an explicit solution for the monopole in the limit of vanishing potential.
    M. K. Prasad and Charles M. Sommerfield, Exact Classical Solution for the 't Hooft Monopole and the Julia-Zee Dyon, Phys. Rev. Lett. 35, 760

  • 1976

    Bogomol'nyi shows that, in the same limit, the second order Euler-Lagrange equations can be reduced to first order equations, now known as the Bogomolny equations or BPS equations.
    E. B. Bogomol'nyi, The Stability of Classical Solutions, Sov. J. Nucl. Phys., Vol 35, No. 4

  • 1977

    Cervero (Harvard University preprint) and Lohe independently establish that the BPS equation on R3 is a reduction of the ASD equation on R4.
    M. A. Lohe, Two- and Three-Dimensional Instantons, Phys. Lett. 70B, No. 3, 325-328

  • 1977

    Manton shows that the scalar and electromagnetic forces between monopoles cancel to leading order, suggesting the existence of static multimonopoles.
    N.S. Manton, The Force Between 't Hooft-Polyakov Monopoles, Nucl. Phys. B. 126, (1977) 525-541

  • 1980

    Jaffe and Taubes prove the existence of multimonopoles, using PDE methods.
    A. Jaffe and C. Taubes, Vortices and monopoles, Birkhäuser (1980)

  • 1981

    Ward and, independently, Forgacs--Horvath--Palla give the first direct construction of a charge 2 monopole, using integrable systems and twistor techniques. This monopole is axially-symmetric.
    Peter Forgács, Zalán Horváth and Lásló Palla, Exact multimonopole solutions in the Bogomolny-Prasad-Sommerfied limit, Physics Letters B 99 (1981) 232--236
    R.S. Ward, A Yang-Mills-Higgs Monopole of Charge 2, Commun. Math. Phys. 79, 317-325 (1981)

  • 1981

    Nahm introduces a construction of monopoles from solutions of and ODE, now called the Nahm equation, by adapting the ADHM technique.
    W. Nahm, All Self-Dual Multimonopoles for Arbitrary Gauge Groups, Cern preprint

  • 1982

    Hitchin introduces the spectral curve associated to a monopole (introducing minitwistor space in the process) and shows it in turn determines the monopole.
    N. J. Hitchin, Monopoles and Geodesics, Commun. Maths. Phys. 83, 579-602

  • 1983

    Hitchin relates Nahm's equation to the monopole spectral curves and thereby shows that Nahm's transform constructs all SU(2) BPS monopoles.
    N.J. Hitchin, On the Construction of Monopoles, Commun. Math. Phys. 89, 145-190 (1983)

  • 1984

    Atiyah introduces the study of monopoles on hyperbolic space, and relates these to instantons and rational maps
    M.F. Atiyah, Magnetic monopoles in hyperbolic spaces, in: Vector bundles on algebraic varieties, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Bombay (1984).

  • 1988

    Atiyah and Hitchin explicitly construct the hyperkähler metric on the moduli space of centred charge 2 monopoles.
    M.F. Atiyah and N.J. Hitchin, The Geometry and Dynamics of Magnetic Monopoles, Princeton University Press (1988)

  • 1989

    Ercolani and Sinha show how to solve Nahm's equations using the Baker-Akhiezer function.
    N. Ercolani and A. Sinha, Monopoles and Baker Functions, Commun. Math. Phys. 125, (1989), 385-416

  • 1983

    Hurtubise and Murray extend the Nahm transform to all classical Lie groups, under the assumption of maximal symmetry-breaking. The formulation of a Nahm transform for the exceptional Lie groups is to this day an open problem.
    Jacques Hurtubise and Michael K. Murray, On the Construction of Monopoles for the Classical Groups, Commun. Math. Phys. 122, 35-89 (1989)

  • 1990

    Braam and Austin exploit the fact that hyperbolic monopoles are determined by their asymptotic connection to give a metric on the space of hyperbolic monopoles.
    Peter J. Braam and David M. Austin, Boundary Values of Hyperbolic Monopoles, Nonlin. 3, 809-823

  • 1994

    Sen conjectures the existence of normalisable harmonic forms on monopole moduli spaces, based on dualities of supersymmetric theories.
    A. Sen, Dyon-monopole bound states, self-dual harmonic forms on the multi-monopole moduli space, and SL(2,Z) invariance in string theory, Physics Letters B 329 (1994) 217-221

  • 1994

    Seiberg and Witten derive the quantum moduli space of N=2 super-Yang-Mills theory, and in particular show that monopoles are responsible for confinement in these gauge theories.
    N Seiberg and E Witten, Monopole Condensation, And Confinement In N=2 Supersymmetric Yang-Mills Theory, Nucl. Phys. B 426:19-52 (1994)
    N Seiberg and E Witten, Monopoles, duality and chiral symmetry breaking in N=2 supersymmetric QCD, Nucl. Phys. B 431:484-550 (1994)

  • 2000

    Hitchin proves Sen's conjectures for the 2-monopole moduli space.
    N. Hitchin, L2 cohomology of hyperkähler quotients, ommun. Math. Phys. 211 (2000) 153--165.

  • 2000

    Cherkis and Kapustin begin the study of periodic monopoles by formulating the Nahm transform.
    S.A. Cherkis and A. Kapustin, Nahm Transform For Periodic Monopoles And N=2 Super Yang-Mills Theory, Commun.Math.Phys. 218 (2001) 333-371

  • 2006

    Bolognesi conjectures that the smallest monopole of fixed charge is shell-like object called a "magnetic bag".
    S. Bolognesi, Multi-monopoles and Magnetic Bags, Nucl.Phys.B752:93-123 (2006)

  • 2012

    Cherkis and Ward formulate the Nahm transform and spectral data for doubly-periodic monopoles, also known as monopole walls.
    S.A. Cherkis and R.S. Ward, Moduli of Monopole Walls and Amoebas, JHEP 1205 (2012) 090

  • 2016

    Moore, Royston and Van den Bleeken formulate make predictions for kernels of Dirac operators on moduli spaces of (singular) monopoles, based on an analysis of framed BPS states with defects.
    G.W. Moore, A.B. Royston and D. Van den Bleeken, Semiclassical framed BPS states, JHEP07 (2016) 071
    G.W. Moore, A.B. Royston and D. Van den Bleeken, L2-Kernels Of Dirac-Type Operators On Monopole Moduli Spaces, arXiv:1512.08923

  • 2018

    Braden and Enolski show how to construct monopoles explicitly using the Nahm transform. In particular, they construct the 2-monopole explicitly.
    H.W. Braden and V.Z. Enolski, The Construction of Monopoles, Commun. Math. Phys. 362, 547–570 (2018)
    H.W. Braden and V.Z. Enolski, The Charge 2 Monopole via the ADHMN construction, Advances in Theoretical and Mathematical Physics 25 (2021) 791-956

  • 2021

    Kottke and Rochon prove Sen's conjecture for the 3-monopole moduli space, using a compactification due to Fritzsch-Kottke-Singer.
    C. Kottke and F. Rochon, L2-cohomology of quasi-fibered boundary metrics, arXiv:2103.16655
